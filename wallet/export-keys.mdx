# Export Private Keys

For security, AceBot never displays private keys in Telegram chat. Instead, a secure external portal is provided for key access.

## Why Keys Aren't Shown in Chat

Displaying private keys in Telegram creates risks:
- **Screenshots** â€” Anyone with access to your phone could capture them
- **Message history** â€” Keys could remain in chat logs
- **Screen recording** â€” Malware could capture the display
- **Shoulder surfing** â€” Someone could see your screen

AceBot's approach protects you from these vectors.

    - View your private key
    - Copy it for backup
    - Export wallet credentials

    </Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## If No Portal Is Available

If the secure portal isn't configured for your instance:
- Contact support through official channels
- Request manual key export assistance
- Never share account details in public channels

## Best Practices for Key Backup

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Store offline" icon="hard-drive">
    Write down your private key on paper or store it on an offline device. Digital storage on connected devices is risky.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use multiple copies" icon="copy">
    Keep backups in multiple secure locations. If one is lost or damaged, you have alternatives.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Never share" icon="user-secret">
    Your private key grants full control of your wallet. Never share it with anyone, including "support" staff.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Consider hardware wallet" icon="microchip">
    For large amounts, consider transferring to a hardware wallet (Ledger, Trezor) for maximum security.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What to Do With Your Private Key

Once you have your private key, you can:

### Import to Other Wallets
Use your key to access the same wallet in:
- MetaMask
- Rabby
- Trust Wallet
- Any EVM-compatible wallet

This doesn't affect your AceBot wallet â€” both can access the same funds.

### Backup for Recovery
If you lose access to your Telegram account:
- Your AceBot account becomes inaccessible
- With your private key, you can recover funds to any wallet

### Migrate to Hardware Wallet
For long-term storage:
1. Set up a hardware wallet
2. Generate a new address
3. Transfer funds from AceBot wallet to hardware wallet
4. Keep small trading amounts in AceBot

## Security Warnings

<Warning>
**Never share your private key with anyone.**

AceBot support will never ask for your private key. Anyone who asks is attempting to steal your funds.
</Warning>

<Warning>
**Verify URLs carefully.**

Phishing sites may impersonate the secure portal. Only access through official AceBot links.
</Warning>

<Warning>
**Use secure devices.**

Access keys only on devices you control, with no malware, on private networks.
</Warning>

## If Your Key Is Compromised

If you suspect someone has access to your private key:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Act immediately">
    Don't wait â€” compromised keys can lead to instant fund loss.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create new wallet">
    Create a new wallet in AceBot immediately.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Transfer funds">
    Move all funds from the compromised wallet to your new wallet as fast as possible.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Delete compromised wallet">
    Remove the old wallet from AceBot once emptied.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Update any integrations">
    If you used the key elsewhere, update those too.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I see my private key in Telegram?">
    No. Private keys are never displayed in Telegram chat for security reasons.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if I lose my Telegram account?">
    Without the private key backed up, you lose access to your wallet. This is why backup is essential.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can AceBot recover my key?">
    Your keys are encrypted with a system secret. Recovery depends on your account access and the specific setup.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is it safe to import my AceBot key elsewhere?">
    Yes. Your key works on any EVM-compatible wallet. Just ensure you're using legitimate wallet software.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
